# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our cybersecurity community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and secure community focused on advancing cybersecurity knowledge and practices.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive cybersecurity environment include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other security professionals
* Being respectful of differing security opinions, methodologies, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive security feedback
* Accepting responsibility for security mistakes and learning from incidents
* Focusing on what is best for the overall cybersecurity community
* Following enterprise security and compliance standards
* Practicing responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities
* Sharing security knowledge for the benefit of all

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, credentials, or security vulnerabilities without permission
* Sharing sensitive security information inappropriately
* Using security tools or knowledge for malicious purposes
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional cybersecurity setting

## Enterprise Cybersecurity Standards

This project maintains enterprise-grade cybersecurity standards:

### Security Standards
* All contributors must follow secure coding practices (OWASP guidelines)
* No sensitive data, credentials, or security keys in public repositories
* All commits must be signed with GPG keys for authenticity
* Security vulnerabilities must be reported through responsible disclosure
* Follow zero-trust security principles
* Implement defense-in-depth strategies

### Quality Standards
* Code must pass comprehensive security scans (SAST, DAST, dependency)
* Security testing and threat modeling required
* Documentation must include security considerations
* Follow established cybersecurity coding conventions
* Maintain enterprise-grade security posture

### Professional Conduct
* Maintain professional cybersecurity communication
* Respect intellectual property and security research ethics
* Follow compliance requirements (SOC 2, ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR)
* Adhere to responsible disclosure practices
* Practice ethical hacking and security research

### Cybersecurity Ethics
* Use security knowledge responsibly and ethically
* Do not engage in malicious activities or unauthorized access
* Respect privacy and data protection principles
* Follow legal and regulatory requirements
* Promote positive cybersecurity practices

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, harmful, or contrary to cybersecurity ethics.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct or cybersecurity best practices.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the cybersecurity community in public spaces. This includes cybersecurity conferences, security research presentations, and professional security forums.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly with appropriate security considerations.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the cybersecurity community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate in a security context.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions that compromise community security standards.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior or security ethics violations.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including malicious use of security knowledge or sustained harassment.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the cybersecurity community.

## Security Incident Reporting

For security-related violations or concerns:
* Follow responsible disclosure practices
* Report security vulnerabilities through appropriate channels
* Do not publicly disclose security issues without proper coordination
* Respect the security and privacy of all community members
